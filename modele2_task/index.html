Chicken

Chicken is more widely consumed than any other meat. Consumers love the taste and practicality; it marries well with a variety of flavors, cooking methods and recipes.Chicken is an affordable protein—much less expensive than beef.* For health conscious individuals, chicken is high in protein; low in calories; rich in essential vitamins like iron, zinc and vitamin B; and low in fat and cholesterol.

Beef

Beef is the meat of cattle (Bos taurus).It is categorized as red meat — a term used for the meat of mammals, which contains higher amounts of iron than chicken or fish.Usually eaten as roasts, ribs, or steaks, beef is also commonly ground or minced. Patties of ground beef are often used in hamburgers.

Sushi

Sushi is a traditional Japanese dish. They look like very simple dish, because it consists only of rice and seafood. However, it should be noted that the European or American recipe is slightly different from the Japanese. After all, in classical sushi cooks put only raw fish. That is why the dish is traditionally consumed with vasabi and ginger - they have strong antibacterial properties.
